Hyderabadi Haleem

Description:

Hyderabadi Haleem is a thick stew originating from Hyderabad, India, often enjoyed during Ramadan. It's made with wheat, lentils, meat, and aromatic spices, slow-cooked to create a rich and creamy texture. Served hot with garnishes like coriander, mint, and fried onions, it's a beloved dish for communal dining and special occasions, known for its flavorful and hearty nature.

Ingredients:

For Haleem:
- 1 cup broken wheat (dalia)
- 1/2 cup whole wheat grains
- 1/2 cup barley
- 500 grams boneless mutton or chicken, cut into small pieces
- 1/2 cup chana dal (split Bengal gram)
- 1/4 cup masoor dal (split red lentils)
- 1/4 cup moong dal (split yellow lentils)
- 1/4 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
- 1 large onion, thinly sliced
- 2 tomatoes, chopped
- 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
- 2 green chilies, slit
- 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
- 1 tablespoon garam masala powder
- Salt to taste
- 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
- 1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped
- 1/2 cup fried onions (for garnish)
- 1/4 cup lemon juice (for garnish)
- Ghee or oil for cooking
- Water as needed

For Spice Bag (Potli):
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 4 green cardamom pods
- 4 cloves
- 1 teaspoon peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf

Instructions:

1. Wash and soak broken wheat, whole wheat grains, and barley together in water for at least 2 hours. Wash and soak all the lentils together in water for at least 1 hour.

2. Tie the spice bag ingredients in a piece of muslin cloth to make a potli.

3. In a large pot or pressure cooker, heat some ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.

4. Add the ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.

5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.

6. Add the mutton or chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they are browned on all sides.

7. Drain the soaked wheat, barley, and lentils. Add them to the pot along with the spice bag (potli), turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Mix well to combine.

8. Add enough water to cover all the ingredients in the pot. If using a pressure cooker, cover and cook for about 4-5 whistles until the meat and grains are tender. If using a regular pot, cover and cook on low heat for about 2-3 hours until everything is cooked and the mixture becomes thick and creamy. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

9. Once the haleem is cooked, remove the spice bag (potli) from the pot and discard.
10. Using a hand blender or a potato masher, blend the cooked mixture until you achieve a smooth and creamy consistency. Add more water if needed to reach your desired thickness.

11. Adjust seasoning if needed and stir in the chopped coriander and mint leaves.

12. Serve hot garnished with fried onions and a drizzle of lemon juice.

Enjoy your delicious Hyderabadi Haleem with naan, roti, or rice! It's a perfect dish for special occasions or as a comforting meal during Ramadan.
