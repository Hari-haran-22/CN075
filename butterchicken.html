Butter Chicken

Description:

Butter Chicken is a popular North Indian dish characterized by tender pieces of chicken cooked in a creamy tomato-based sauce. It's known for its rich and velvety texture, mildly spiced flavors, and vibrant orange-red color. The chicken is typically marinated in yogurt and spices before being cooked in a sauce made from butter, tomatoes, cream, and various spices such as garam masala, cumin, and fenugreek.

Ingredients:

For Marinating Chicken:

- 500 grams boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1/2 cup plain yogurt
- 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
- Salt to taste

For the Sauce:

- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 tomatoes, pureed
- 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
- 1 teaspoon coriander powder
- 1 teaspoon cumin powder
- 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 1 tablespoon honey or sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
- Salt to taste
- Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
- Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. In a bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt.
Add the chicken pieces and coat them well with the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or up to 4 hours.
2. Heat 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.

3. In the same pan, add the remaining butter and oil. Add the chopped onion and sauté until they turn soft and translucent.

4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.

5. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture and it thickens.

6. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and garam masala powder. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the spices are well incorporated into the sauce.

7. Lower the heat and stir in the heavy cream. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously, until the sauce is creamy and smooth.

8. Add honey or sugar if desired to balance the acidity of the tomatoes. Adjust salt to taste.

9. Add the cooked chicken pieces to the sauce and mix until they are evenly coated. Simmer for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and kasuri methi (if using).

11. Serve hot with naan, rice, or roti.

Enjoy your homemade Butter Chicken, a perfect blend of creamy and spicy flavors!
