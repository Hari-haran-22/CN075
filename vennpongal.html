Venn Pongal

Description:

Venn Pongal is a traditional South Indian dish made from rice and lentils, typically enjoyed for breakfast or as a comfort food. It's cooked together with cumin seeds, black pepper, ginger, and curry leaves, giving it a flavorful and aromatic taste. Once cooked to a creamy consistency, it's often garnished with cashews, peanuts, and ghee (clarified butter). Venn Pongal is served hot and pairs well with coconut chutney and sambar, making it a beloved dish in South Indian cuisine.

Ingredients:

- 1 cup raw rice (preferably short-grain rice like sona masuri)
- 1/2 cup split yellow moong dal (yellow lentils)
- 4 cups water
- 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
- 1 tablespoon oil
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
- 1 inch piece of ginger, finely chopped
- 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
- 1/4 cup cashew nuts (optional)
- A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
- Salt to taste
- Freshly chopped coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. Rinse the rice and moong dal separately under running water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.

2. In a pressure cooker or a heavy-bottomed pot, add the rice and moong dal along with 4 cups of water. Pressure cook for about 3-4 whistles or cook in a pot until the rice and dal are soft and mushy. Set aside.

3. Heat ghee and oil together in a pan over medium heat.

4. Add cumin seeds and black peppercorns. Let them splutter.

5. Add chopped ginger, green chilies, and cashew nuts (if using). Saut√© until the cashews turn golden brown.

6. Add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) and mix well.

7. Transfer the cooked rice and dal mixture into the pan with the tempering. Mix well to combine.

8. Add salt to taste and mix thoroughly.

9. If the pongal appears too thick, you can adjust the consistency by adding a little hot water.

10. Allow the pongal to simmer for a few minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reaches your desired consistency.

11. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves, if desired.

12. Serve Venn Pongal hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or gotsu (a South Indian curry made with vegetables).

Enjoy your delicious and comforting Venn Pongal!
