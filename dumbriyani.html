Dum Briyani

Description:

Dum Biryani is a fragrant rice dish from South Asia and the Middle East. It features marinated meat layered with partially cooked rice and aromatic spices. Slow-cooked over low heat (dum), it yields tender meat and flavorful rice, served hot and garnished with fried onions and fresh herbs. It's a beloved dish for special occasions, known for its rich flavors.

Ingredients:

- 2 cups basmati rice
- 500g chicken (or your choice of meat/vegetables)
- 1 cup plain yogurt
- 2 onions, thinly sliced
- 3 tomatoes, chopped
- 2-3 green chilies, slit
- 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh coriander leaves
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
- Whole spices: 2-3 bay leaves, 4-5 cloves, 4-5 green cardamoms, 2-3 cinnamon sticks, 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon red chili powder
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
- 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
- Salt to taste
- 4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
- Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional)
- Fried onions for garnish (optional)

- For Dum:

- Dough made from wheat flour (to seal the pot)
- 1/4 cup milk
- 1/4 cup water
- Few drops of kewra water or rose water (optional)

Instructions:

1.Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.

2. Marinate the chicken (or meat/vegetables) with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, half of the chopped coriander leaves, half of the chopped mint leaves, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.

3. Heat ghee in a large heavy-bottomed pot. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.

4. Add the whole spices and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.

5. Add the marinated chicken (or meat/vegetables) along with chopped tomatoes. Cook until the chicken is partially cooked and tomatoes are soft.

6. In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Add salt and the soaked rice. Cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked. Drain and set aside.

7. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture in the pot. Sprinkle garam masala powder, remaining chopped coriander leaves, and mint leaves over the rice.

8. Drizzle saffron milk over the rice for a beautiful color and aroma. You can also add fried onions for additional flavor at this stage.

9. Prepare the dough and use it to seal the edges of the pot.

10. Mix milk, water, and kewra water (or rose water) and pour it gently over the rice.

11. Cover the pot tightly with a lid and place it on a tawa (griddle) over low heat. Let it cook on dum (steam) for about 20-25 minutes until the rice and chicken are completely cooked and aromatic.

12. Once done, remove the pot from heat and let it sit for another 5-10 minutes before opening the lid.

13. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork and serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or salad.

Enjoy your delicious homemade dum biryani!
